Diploma in Horticulture

Comprehensive training in plant cultivation, landscape management, and sustainable agriculture

About

A diploma in Horticulture provides students with in-depth knowledge of plant cultivation, soil management, landscaping, and sustainable agricultural practices. This program prepares students for careers in gardening, agribusiness, and environmental conservation.

Key Components

  • Plant Biology: Understanding plant growth, physiology, and genetics.
  • Soil and Fertilization: Soil science, composting, and nutrient management.
  • Landscape Design: Planning and maintaining gardens, parks, and green spaces.
  • Pest and Disease Control: Managing plant health using organic and scientific methods.
  • Greenhouse Management: Techniques for controlled environment farming.

Practical Training

  • Internships in nurseries, botanical gardens, and farms.
  • Hands-on experience in plant propagation and soil testing.
  • Workshops on organic farming, hydroponics, and landscape planning.

Career Opportunities

  • Landscape Designer
  • Nursery Manager
  • Agricultural Consultant
