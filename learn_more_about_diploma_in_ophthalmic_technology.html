Diploma in Ophthalmic Technology

Comprehensive training in eye care, vision testing, and ophthalmic procedures

About

A diploma in Ophthalmic Technology trains students in the fundamentals of eye care, including vision testing, ophthalmic instruments, and assisting ophthalmologists in clinical settings. The program provides both theoretical knowledge and practical experience in the field of optometry and eye health.

Key Components

  • Ophthalmic Procedures: Basics of eye examinations and vision testing.
  • Optical Instrumentation: Training on handling and maintaining ophthalmic instruments.
  • Ocular Diseases: Understanding common eye disorders and their management.
  • Patient Care: Techniques for assisting patients with eye-related issues.
  • Refraction and Contact Lenses: Training in prescribing and fitting corrective lenses.

Practical Training

  • Internships in hospitals, eye clinics, and optical centers.
  • Hands-on experience with refraction tests and contact lens fitting.
  • Exposure to surgical procedures and ophthalmic imaging techniques.
